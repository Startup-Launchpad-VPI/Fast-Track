01 · The bigger picture

Why we back founders

EPFL backs founders as a strategic investment, not charity and not a public service. Turning research and talent into companies is how value, capability and jobs stay in Switzerland. The support exists to de-risk a venture to the point where it can reach its next real milestone: the first investor conversation worth having, the first pilot customer, the first competitive grant from outside EPFL. It is a bridge to that point, not a destination. The aim is to make founders ready to no longer need it.

That is where you come in. Your experience is a multiplier on the founder's own work, not a substitute for it. The four roles below are different ways to apply it, from a single sharp hour to a few months of companionship.

Fast Track is EPFL's route for fast-to-market ventures. It spans three programs: the Blaze Accelerator, the AI Track, and the Master Project in your Startup (PDM). Deep-tech ventures follow a separate Deep Track (Innogrants, Ignition grants), so the founders you'll meet here are building for rapid market entry, not long research timelines.

The line we hold

We help founders increase validation so they reach a clearer, less-risky roadmap to scale, or kill the wrong project fast before more time is spent on it. Both are wins. The only failure is staying busy in the middle.

03 · Pressure-testing · Red Team

Red Team

A standing challenge engine: experienced operators who red-team founders on the assumptions that actually carry weight. Not mentorship, not validation. Pressure-testing.

We match each founder to a challenger with the right scar tissue, run one focused session, and our coaches hold the continuity so the challenge converts into follow-through instead of evaporating. The goal isn't to demolish founders. It's to surface the weakest load-bearing assumption before the market or daily operations do it for them.

How a session runs

BEAT 1

Where they say they're going

The founder lays out the destination and the claim behind it.

BEAT 2

What that takes

You bring the reality: the real cost, the real timeline, the conditions that have to hold.

BEAT 3

What that means today

You translate it into the decisions they should make now, with what they actually have.

What we ask of you

  • Read a one-page brief and an initial profile review before the session.
  • Run one focused session, 60–90 min, remote. Direct, specific, adversarial in the productive sense.
  • Sign an NDA before any session. Founder material is pre-launch and sensitive.
  • Send a short, structured candid note to the program afterwards on how solid and how coachable the founder looks. It stays between you and us, never shared with the founder, so the session stays honest.

What you don't do

  • Ongoing mentorship, hand-holding, or pitching the founder anything.
Token & recognition

200 CHF per session, as a token of recognition rather than a market rate for your time. After two sessions you can publicly mention being part of the EPFL Startup Launchpad red team.

The challenge domains

Each case names one or two of these as the priority:

  • Technology state-of-the-art — is the claimed edge real and defensible?
  • Technology vs. product — what is shippable vs. what is a research bet?
  • Product design — does the thing solve the problem people actually have?
  • B2B & institutional sales — who buys, why now, and through what motion?
  • Financial forecast — what has to be true for the numbers to hold?
Who we're looking for

Operators who think natively in zero-to-one terms: builders, not pure managers. If reconciling a big claim with an empty starting position sounds energizing rather than annoying, you're who we want.

04 · Sustained support · Blue Team

Coaches

The program's companion for a team. You walk a venture through structured de-risking over a few months, using a shared readiness framework, so progress is real and visible.

Format & commitment

Rhythm
7 to 10 check-in sessions of 60–90 min, mostly bi-weekly, across a 3 to 6 month window. Remote (in person is possible, but expenses are not covered or facilitated).
Load
1 to 3 teams in parallel, your choice.
Per team
About 18 hours: a 1.5–2h initial team profiling, the check-in sessions, a 1h one-third pulse check with the program manager, a 1.5h mid-term review, and a 1.5h closing session. Plus a one-time 1h intro call before your first team.
Reporting
A 5-minute pulse-check form to the program team after each session.

What we ask of you

  • Keep the team actively de-risking their project, and strengthen their use of the framework.
  • Make sure they have the resources to do the work, and that they feel genuinely supported and motivated.
  • Give the program visibility on progress against the team's defined objectives, or flag disengagement early.
Compensation

2,000 CHF per team. This is a mandate, not an employment contract: you are responsible for your own taxes and contributions. No extras.

Recognition

  • After a short induction (your first 5 sessions) you can publicly mention the program and your role, with guidelines provided, and we endorse it.
  • Active-role mention pauses if you go more than 6 months without supporting a team.
  • Active coaches are listed on the website and invited to all Launchpad events.
  • A signed NDA is required for every team.
Who we're looking for · draft

Founders or operators who have built something real and can hold a standard without lecturing. Comfortable with the de-risk-or-kill logic, equally able to challenge and to support, and reliable on cadence. You receive a coach handbook with the full method before you start.

05 · Spot expertise · Blue Team

Advisors

One-off, high-leverage help when your specific profile matches a team's specific challenge. You bring clarity and, where you can, an asset the team can't easily reach.

Format & commitment

  • One 60-minute session, on-call. We contact you when a relevant challenge appears, the same matchmaking model as the Red Team.
  • Accountability is built in. The team documents the follow-up actions and you validate them at the end of the call.

What you provide

  • Clarity, insight, honest and constructive feedback, and your personal opinion on strategic questions.
  • Where possible, a personal introduction to a relevant contact, or access to a useful asset.

What you don't do

  • Your commitment is bounded by the session and what you offer in it. You're free to do no further follow-up.
  • No endorsement of the project.
Want to keep advising afterwards?

Just contact us to clarify status. It can stay pro-bono within the program, or move outside it if there's an investment interest. In that case terms are between you and the founders, and the program does not support that arrangement.

Recognition

  • Listed in the advisor pool on the website.
  • Can be invited to speak, and invited to all Launchpad events.
  • A signed NDA is required.
Who we're looking for

Successful founders and entrepreneurs, top technical profiles, executives, business and strategy consultants, senior people in relevant industries, and investors. Senior, with noticeable achievements or a high level of expertise.

06 · Inspiration & expertise

Speakers

Two kinds of voice: founders sharing an honest personal story, and professionals sharing hard-won expertise.

Format & commitment

  • Launchpad talks and EPFL startup events. Format and setting vary; you decide what works for you, and can suggest your own.
  • A single talk or a recurring presence, your call.
  • Audience is open, but the focus is creating value for EPFL founders, entrepreneurs and the wider community.
Compensation & recognition

No specific fee; for larger-scope events it can be discussed. You get high visibility on our channels, and potentially a place in the founders club if there's mutual interest.

Who we're looking for

The same calibre as our advisors, plus a real desire to share a message and have impact.
